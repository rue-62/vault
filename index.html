How it works

Everything runs in your browser — no data is sent anywhere, ever.

Your passphrase drives PBKDF2-SHA256 (600 000 iterations) to derive a unique key. Every encryption uses a fresh random salt and IV, so identical text produces different output each time.

AES-256-GCM both encrypts and authenticates: a wrong passphrase fails loudly rather than silently producing garbage. The output is self-contained — copy it anywhere.

Use a passphrase you can memorize but not guess. Four or five unrelated words strung together works well.
